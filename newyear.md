# 2020: A Year Felt Like Ages

While our youthful eyes are eagerly waiting to see beautified and decorated sky-scrapers, malls, showrooms, and Christmas trees, and whatnot, as New-year’s Eve is just a few days away, we also want this famously disastrous year 2020 to see an end. It is written nowhere that with 2021, all our problems will abandon us, still, let us just believe that the new year will bring an improved and comforting tomorrow for you and me.

To be honest, it was undoubtedly Coronavirus which took and spoiled mercilessly most part of the ongoing year, eating out all the festivals with no parties…and most painfully, no theatrical releases of films. However, with a lot of difficulties, of course, can’t we try to find out a bit of the positive side of 2020, too?☺

## A Few Firsts for Some of Us, May Be?

2020 was all cheery and normal and fresh for most of us till February, I suppose. During those couple of months, some of you might have found your first job, or a new hobby, a very first and dreamy romance, or the technically third yet first real romance of your lives. Although all those ‘new’ stuff might have seen unprecedented consequences after the spread of Coronavirus. In fact, on the negative side, news were making rounds that the lockdown caused a few relationships to face premature deaths.

On the other hand, a few like me realized that we kept considering our life as a race wherein it was actually a journey. Life, which should be a joy ride, became all about study, then marks, then admission and then job and then the so-called “social institution” chiefly guided by parents and far-end relatives (yes...we are talking about typical Indian arranged marriages here!!).

This whole thing has helped us, or at least me to sit down for a while and do nothing much!! Yes! You read that right. It is completely okay to take a break and be less productive (in economical terms, of course) for a certain while. There is a saying in Italian “Dolce far niente” that literally means “the art of doing nothing!!”

Our mind and body both, which kept consuming positives and negatives from the outer world, also need time and space to process the stuff and clear out the unnecessary burden that it has been carrying for years. Even machines stop performing after a point, so our own human body legit deserves some rest and pampering. All in all, it is okay, rather, desirable to take some more ‘chill pills.’ Just for clarification, here, chill pills include some books, some web-series, some chocolates, and lots and lots of sleep and cuddles if you have such an option. 😉

## Major discounts on OTT and Online Learning Platforms:

What was our first reaction towards a complete and strict lockdown at that time? We panicked. Some in a little amount while some must have gone beyond furious with China and the global situation. The next thing most of us must have at least tried was to utilize the time. While yoga, or diet, or “studying for exams” is not everyone’s cup of tea, entertainment was everyone’s go-to arena.

Despite crashing economies and sluggish markets, those platforms which largely rely upon the internet have bloomed over the last 6-7 months. Online learning platforms such as Udemy and many others had given notable discounts at the time to master specific skills.

OTT platforms like Netflix are still in demand on a huge level due to their content and people’s free time made these platforms to achieve heights. Closed cinema theatres and minimum outings have forced us to find pleasures within the four walls only.

However, we cannot completely ignore the negative factors of this tricky dice called Pandemic. Here is another perspective for the same time period.

## 2020: The Corona Phase:

Some of us are still astonished to see how the world around us has turned upside down lately. One of the most off-putting things we all must have realized is that we won't be able to see the full faces of people on the streets and public places, at least till the Corona vaccine makes its way to us! The charm of suddenly bumping into a random friend or relative has vanished in the last 10 months.

It was March 2020 when Coronavirus entered our country, apparently, because of China, and literally created havoc. Healthcare experts are still struggling to find a solution to it. In this scenario, even world leaders such as ex-US President Trump with the ex-First Lady Melania Trump, UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson, and our very own Minister of Home Affairs Amit Shah got infected with Covid-19.

Those who had 9 to 6 kinds of desk jobs, especially in private sectors like me had to face quite a lot of troubles and discomforts in the initial stages of lockdown. While some of us had even lost jobs, most of the employees had to go with the ‘work from home’ method even though it took a hefty toll on their health.

Honestly, work from home happens to be the most tedious practice, if it applies for multiple months. It takes away the cheerfulness of chattering while having coffee with your colleagues. You start missing the fun part of being ‘in the office’ while the workload never leaves you. In this spell, spinal cord related disorders, like back and neck pain saw a massive rise due to this way of working.

On the other hand, catastrophic impacts occurred to the global economy, especially after the infamous virus started harming robust economies like Italy, the US, and India. Stock markets hit the rock bottom before they could initiate minimal recovery.

Doctors must have also witnessed that these frequent spells of lockdowns have affected our mental wellness too. Staying away from sunlight and an absolute lack of social meetings have caused each one of us to enter a shell where we don’t get to share our feelings, or experiences, be it traumatizing or pleasant. In a country like ours, where mental wellness is a less-discussed subject, it becomes all the more difficult for patients to deal with reality.

Well, in this scenario, let us just hope that we won’t have to sacrifice our beloved festivals like Navratri in the upcoming year. ☺☺
