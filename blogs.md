# Mental Health in India: The Hassle, the Hope, and the Hush Hush!
When I was in college around the year 2016, there was a very promising, cheerful classmate whose uncle was the Dept. Head. In a casual conversation, she admitted that she takes antidepressants (under a psychiatrist’s treatment). She seemed nothing but confident. We all agreed that it was not something “shocking” or “ashaming.”

In the same week, another student (let’s call her ‘A’), from the same college, with whom I was traveling, quarreled with her friend as she had leaked the fact that ‘A’ takes anti-depressants. ‘A’ was crying uncontrollably as she felt betrayed by her friend because she let the class know about her medication!! 

…Two very different approaches regarding the same disorder. 

At that time, my mom was also dealing with chronic OCD while me and my sister were busy finishing our education and taking care of mom like the rest of our family. 

2-3 years later, I was working full-time, and had nothing huge to complain about, and then 2020 came, bringing the horrendous pandemic. Here, I got diagnosed with GAD i.e. Generalized Anxiety Disorder. 

Since then, many external things have altered, including my surroundings, usual worries, and my marital status. With that, the internal struggles and insecurities also took turns, while the extent of the struggle also escalated.

What makes millennial and/or menopausal women so much more vulnerable to depression, anxiety, and/or mental exhaustion? Looks like patriarchy has been doing its job too well and too heavily (with a bit of help from capitalism). 
—---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

India, a country with a vast number of cultures and languages, unites on a handful of things; one is romanticizing ‘tolerance,’ and ‘struggles,’ the second is hardcore misogyny, almost to the point of slavery; another is widespread ignorance. The poisonous cocktail of these three aspects is playing a key role in deteriorating the perception of mental health.

On the other hand, rising numbers of people consulting psychiatric professionals have somewhat stirred healthy and much-needed conversations regarding mental and emotional wellness. However, such conversations and efforts have reached only so far, as a majority of people, including middle-aged masses and people from less fortunate backgrounds, choose to remain in their rigid bubble. 

Unfortunately, such bubbles consist of strongly harmful religious beliefs and social pressure only, which they pass down to their younger generation. Those who resist such stuff become victims of different degrees of hostile behavior, especially women. 

## Ignorance

“It’s all in your head…you’re just fine…look at us, we did this, we did that; we don't have depression viprassion!!” Exact words of 99% percent of households.

Sometimes it’s a lack of basic empathy, other times, it is the denial mode towards one’s mental struggle. In a comparatively healthy set-up, immediate family members including parents, spouses, or siblings, might want to help; however, fail to do so since their knowledge regarding such issues can be limited. 

Some of us do have educated and empathetic parents who offer genuine solutions like medical help and therapy. Unfortunately, these are rare sets of parents. 

Considering the general ignorance regarding common mental illnesses like depression, anxiety, hyper-stress, and OCD, mental health education is the new sex education!!

## Malnutrition

Malnutrition, largely a result of poverty and unemployment, is a great cause behind the malfunctioning of the human brain. Deficiencies of vitamins such as vitamin B complex, vitamin D, Omega 3, folic acid, and so-and-so-forth at a young age can be a reason behind the onset of mental health problems.

Your brain and emotional health, along with many other bodily functions, depend upon neurons (nerves). These N numbers of nerves play a vital part in all your brain functions, sense of happiness, sense of calm, sadness or disappointment, and fear. All the above-mentioned vitamins are necessary for nerves to remain thriving. 

The nervous system, in tandem with hormones like serotonin, melatonin, oxytocin, dopamine, and a handful more, is crucial for mental health. 

A balanced diet that is full of nutrition is one of the basic needs for a healthy brain; the other basic need is a sense of calm in life. Constant chaos keeps a person in constant survival mode, making him/her unable to process emotions.

## Lack of Resources

If at all you try to find a therapist or a psychiatrist, it will be a journey till you find ‘the one’ that can really help you. In a country where fake degree-holding doctors are easier to find than a safe public toilet, seeking suitable and cost-friendly psychiatric help without a lot of fuss is still a fantasy.

Let’s believe that you found the correct therapist. Now, the cost of therapy can be a bit of a shock, as in cities like Delhi or Mumbai, the per-session fee starts from 2200 INR. There are a few entities (like ‘the friendly couch’) that try to provide therapy at a lesser price; however, the lack of qualified therapists affects the sphere of cost.

## Fear of Judgment

Imagine an individual with an anxious tendency to go on a date (as if a healthy dating culture exists in India!!). What are the chances that he or she will reveal that she has anxiety in the initial stage of their courtship?

Even some of our own generation’s folks easily make fun of mental illnesses without really meaning any harm, let alone the previous generations. 

Especially, the medication required for such disorders is looked upon with some serious disapproval. Plus, very few uncles and aunties have learned to mind their own business in our nation. The fear of judgment worsens the situation here, as this factor oftentimes overpowers one’s will to get better and seek the right treatment.

## The Strange Combination of Lack of Privacy + No One to Talk To

Nail in the coffin!! It's a crowded country, where an individual might have to struggle to find time and space for one’s self, to ground nerves and pursue calm. 

It is very tough for a legit married Indian couple to exist like a couple (let alone the unmarried ones). The concept of large families staying together kills everything in you, that makes you ‘You.’ Even today, parents or in-laws choose to act like ‘supervisors’ to their adult children and their spouse. 

Despite being around so many people simultaneously, you rarely connect with one; this makes life much harder than it is. One of the basic human needs is to be able to connect; often, to be able to connect, you need harmony, and harmony never comes from ‘Supervision.’

It is astonishing how certain ‘big families’ have zero respect for one’s private space; like they don’t even feel uncomfortable while invading someone’s privacy.

Re-shaped Misogyny

Around five decades ago, it was tough to become a double-graduated working woman; people would show a sense of surprise at such an occurrence. Nowadays, there are plenty of families out there expecting their wives or DILs to earn a sufficient amount of money ‘to contribute’ after laboring into so-called household duties. Even if you fulfill their expectations, oftentimes, ruthless verbal attacks are thrown towards the daughter-in-law.

No wonder, those married women’s bodies are riddled with various types of backaches and endless mental exhaustion. Marriage which should be a partnership at its core, is becoming the strongest tool of patriarchy, sheer inequality, and straight-out torture.

Amid this, slowly young women of India are choosing to remain single till at least their late twenties, or sometimes forever. However, being single has its own share of issues like difficulty in finding a place to stay, and social disapproval. 

## Educational Pressure

At an alarmingly high frequency, students taking higher education commit suicide in India. No amount of motivational speeches can alleviate the pressure that is put on the students by the education system and society. How disheartening is it to witness young adults taking their own lives without even experiencing the life itself!!

Instead of labeling students or young adults with mental struggles ‘sensitive’ what we can do is put efforts towards creating a healthy society that doesn’t treat women and children like secondary members. Moreover, improving psychiatric help and making it more affordable for everyone should be the primary goal.
